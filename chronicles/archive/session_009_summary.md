## Narrative Summary
Following the brutal encounter in the granary cellar, Wael and Kaelen retreated to the Boar and Barrel for a brief, tense rest. Wael used this time to process the trauma of his injury and stabilize his physical condition.

Seeking more permanent care, they returned to the Oakhaven Chapel. Father Vane, recognizing the severity of the "rat-dog" bite and the potential for rot, performed a focused medical treatment. Through a combination of mundane skill and divine guidance, Vane purged the infection from Wael's left eye, though the sight remains lost and the scarring permanent.

In the quiet aftermath within the sanctuary, Wael turned his focus to his craft. He began designing "The Mason's Blinder," a specialized leather and metal eye-patch. The design evolved from a simple covering into a sophisticated artisan's tool, incorporating a mounting point for a magnifying crystal to compensate for his lost depth perception. The session concludes with the design finalized, awaiting materials for construction.
